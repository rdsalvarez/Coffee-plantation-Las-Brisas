Pacamara

Pacamara coffee variety is a coffee tree that originates in 1958 from EL Salvador. It took approximately 30 years of scientific investigation to develop this variety. It results from the crossing between Pacas and Maragogipe varieties. Its cultivated in EL Salvador were with frequency dominates the Cup of Excellence tournaments. It takes three years to start producing and it should be cultivated at high altitudes (1,680m to 1,750 m).

Pacamara coffee has a medium body and high acidity. It has red fruit and citric taste. It also has chocolaty fragrance. Its aroma is intense, has medium body and creammy texture. Posseses Sweet notes of chocolate and butter and hints of citrus flavors

The coffee tree is medium size with alot of foliage, and big size leaves. Its size is compact that permits more trees to be grown in small spaces. It has a big oval seed size.
