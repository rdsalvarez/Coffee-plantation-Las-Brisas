History

Las Brisas (Breezes in English) is the name of the plantation that grows coffee in El Salvador, Central America. It is located on the southwest part of the country a few miles from a small colonial town called Apaneca aproximatly 1 and Â½ hour from San Salvador, capital city of El Salvador.

The earliest known reference by Spanish monk inhabitants of this small town is 1550. The name Apaneca, Apanejecat in Indian nahuat language, means rivers of wind or sea of wind. Justifiably Apaneca is located at 1,450 meters above sea level and during the months of December to March, strong winds blow lowering temperatures in the region.

The plantation of 72 acres, was acquired in 1882 by Mr. Leon Borja of Spanish descent. Since then coffee has been grown through time by its descendants: Dr. Guillermo Borja original owner, her daughter, Mrs. Isabel Borja, his grandson Dr. Arturo Alvarez Borja and today his great grandson Rafael Alvarez Schneider.

The plantation is divided in 6 plots or areas that range from 5 acres to 12 acres. It grows and produces Bourbon (33%), Pacamara (30%) , and Catymor (32%) varieties.

With the presence of coffee rust and climate change a different strategy had to be taken for the business to survive. Since stock prices vary constantly, mostly downwards, a decision was made to grow and produce mostly quality coffee focusing in planting Bourbon and Pacamara varieties.

Two families reside on the farm: The father and his family which have resided for more than 40 years, and his son and his family who have lived there for almost 30 years. Notwithstanding coffee agriculture needs, both families are made sure to have work provided all year round.

Year round coffee activities can be summarized in: pruning, fertilizing (2 or 3 a year), weed control (2 or 3 a year), tree shadow pruning, fungus control (2 or 3 a year) and finally harvest.
