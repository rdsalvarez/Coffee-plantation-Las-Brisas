Harvest 

  Las Brisas coffee plantation's harvest usually starts at the end 
  of the rainy season in other words late November or beginning 
  of December and it lasts until middle of March.

  During this stage, the plantation employs around 15 people. Its a 
  family activity were children help their parents pick the coffee 
  cheries.

  The main objective of the picking process is to choose the cherries which 
  have a deep red to purplish color. These cherries have the right 
  amount of sugars and a type of nectar. They are at a optimum 
  stage of picking. These are the best cherries which have the highest 
  quality properties sought by coffee drinkers. 

  Harvest process starts early in the morning about 7am and ends around 3
  in the afternoon. The harvesters utilize wicker baskets were they deposit
  the picked cherries. After collecting cherries a selection process is made
  were unmature berries, sticks, pebles, ect are taken out.

  After the selection process, the coffee is deposited in sacs were they 
  will be weighed and record accordingly to what each person 
  or group has picked.

  The coffee sacs are all weighed and are transported to the mill were 
  they will undergo several transformations to produce the best 
  quality coffee.
