Shade tree prunning 

  The purpose of shade tree prunning includes increase coffee 
  production, regulate microclimate, regulate radiation and 
  increase nutritional water balance.

  Tree shade prunning begins at the beginning of rainy season.
  The types of tree prunning are formation and maintenance. The 
  tools used for this tasks are machetes, prunning saws and 
  chainsaws.

 
  Shade from the trees in the interior of the coffee 
  plantation has several functions:

    - Conserve soil humidity
    - Return soil nutrients
    - Regulate soil and root temperature
    - Reduce water evaporation from the soil
    - Reduce plant transpiration
    - Hinder weed grownth
    - Reduce erosion
    - Protect coffee from strong winds
    - Reduce damages because of low temperatures
    - Regulate the control of pests and diseases

  Shade trees are the trees that live together with coffee, 
  providing shade during all the productive cycle.

  Shade trees have the following caracteristics:

    - Good grownth, development and longevity
    - Good light distribution and filtration
    - Deep root
    - Wind resistant
    - Tree foliage conservation all year round
    - Abundant ramification
    - Easy management
    - Good and rapid regeneration capacity
    - Provide nitrogen fixation


In the beginning of summer, the property experiences natural
winds damaging the coffee trees. Because of this, the farm is 
surrounded of protective windbreaks of trees. Because they also 
generate shade and impede coffee production, they have to 
be trimmed every three years.
