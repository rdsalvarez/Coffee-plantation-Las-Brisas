Disease and pest control

ROYA

Coffee trees are delicate as we humans are. They require special and careful attention to grow adequatly. Because of their vulnerability they are very susceptible to pests and disease.

The main disease of coffee tree is a fungus named Roya (Hemileia vastatrix). Its a devastating disease and it mainly affects the leaves. It manifests itself as yellow or orange pustules on the inferior part of the leaves.

These injuries provoke the premature fall of the leaves, reducing the fotosynthesis capacity of the plant and as a result it diminishes its yield. The defoliation weakens the plant and reduces the quality of the grains.

This disease appears mostly in humid climates. The main way of reducing its impact on the plant is thru spraying with fungicides and trying to maintain a healthy plant. Other methods include planting varieties that are resistant to this disease.


OJO DE GALLO

Another fungus (Mycena citricolor) which attacks the coffee tree produces circular brown or gray spots that will defoliate the plant too. As with the fungus roya, it will diminish the fotosynthetic capacity of the plant which in turn will diminish the coffee yields and quality. Use of fungicide spraying helps combat this disease.


BROCA

This beetle (Hypothenemus hampei) feeds of the beans of coffee. It perforates the bean to lay its eggs. The larva that emerge feed off the grain, destroying the grain and signifacntly reduce the quality of the grain. The control of this pest increases the costs of production, which in turn increases coffee prices to consumers.

NEMATODES

Nematodes are soil parasites that attack the roots of coffee plants causing the formation of nodules. This damage limits the roots capacity to absorb water and nutrients, weakening the tree and reducing its yields.

The damage caused by this parasites are particularly dangerous on young plants, were they could easily dye. The management of this pests is a challange and often requires a combination of cultural practices, use of resistant varieties and in some cases aplication of nematicides.
