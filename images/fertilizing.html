Fertilizing

One of the most important crops in the world is coffee. Its productivity depends mainly in a adequate fertilization. Its essential that the appropriate fertilizars are applied to guarantee grownth of healthy coffee trees that will produce high quality grains.

Diferent types of fertilizers are used in coffee production. Each one has its caracteristics and benefits: organic, quemical, of slow release, and foliar fertilizers.

Organic fertilizers are obtained thru organic materials, like compost, manure or residues from crops. Its a sustainable option which respects the environment. They help improve the structure of the soil, improve water retention and promote beneficial microbial activity.

Quemical fertilizers contain specific nutrients that are formed quemically. The result is inmediate and you can apply more precise dosages of nutrients. However, the excesive use, can generate unbalances and damage the health of the soil.

Fertilizers of slow release are designed to react gradually releasing nutrients thru time. They provide constant nutrients to the plant and reduce the loss by leaching. These fertilizers are ideal for soils which have low nutrient retention capacity.

Foliar fertilizers are applied directly to the leaves of the plants. They are rapidly absorbed and utilized by the plants, which converts them in a efficient option to correct nutritional deficiencies or adapt to situations of plant stress.

Which fertilizer to apply and what quantity? That will depend on the type of soil, the nutritional requirements of the plant, and the climate conditions. The best thing to do is to do periodic soil and foliar analysis to evaluate nutrient requirements.

In El Salvador, a month after harvest, you can start applying foliar sprays. Generally speaking, chemical fertilizers are applied as soon as rainy season is set.
